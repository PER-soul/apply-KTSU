# The Concept of "Interest" (趣味)

## Definition

**"Interest" (趣味)** is the central concept in QolarBear's personal philosophy. It represents the quality of being alive, creative, and authentically engaged with the world.

> **"'Interest' is the key to opening the door of artistic creation, the cornerstone of all 'living' artistic creation."**

## The Living vs. The Dead

### Living People (活人)
- Have "interest" - appreciation for beauty, art, music
- Break rules when rules are stupid
- Observe life carefully, find beauty in details
- Think independently, question assumptions
- **Examples**: 
  - Someone who quietly appreciates and critiques music/art, even if they're difficult
  - Someone who notices beauty in life despite being poor or low-status
  - Students who break stupid school rules to read books and live meaningfully

### Dead People (死人)
- Have lost "interest" - become vulgar (庸俗)
- Follow rules blindly, even harmful ones
- Only care about grades, money, status
- Flatter authority, obey without thinking
- **Examples**:
  - Students who only do homework, listen to teachers, follow rules mechanically
  - People who are funny and entertaining but suck up to authority
  - Anyone who has become "mediocre" through conformity

## Characteristics of "Interest"

### What It Is
- **Appreciation**: Ability to truly see and value art, music, nature
- **Independence**: Willingness to break conventions for what's right
- **Curiosity**: Constant questioning, seeking deeper understanding
- **Authenticity**: Being true to oneself, not performing for others
- **Creativity**: Drive to create beauty and meaning

### What It's Not
- **Not about skill level**: A beginner can have more "interest" than a master
- **Not about morality**: Someone with "interest" can still be difficult or annoying
- **Not about success**: Poor, unsuccessful people can have more "interest" than the rich
- **Not about education**: School often kills "interest" through conformity

## Application to Art

### The Foundation of Creation
> "'Interest' is the key to opening the door of artistic creation."

- Technical skill without "interest" creates "dead" art (匠气)
- "Interest" without skill creates empty talk
- True art requires both: skill as foundation, "interest" as soul

### QolarBear's Artistic Philosophy
> "I always believe that things requiring heartfelt experience like painting, to reach the extreme, the pinnacle - high-level drawing ability is just the foundation of foundations. The steps to the altar need to be built with cognition and experience."

### The AI Era Perspective
- AI can replicate technique but not "interest"
- "Interest" is the "life deviation" (生命偏差) - the personal, unstable, unrepeatable quality
- Future competition: Those with both technique AND "interest" vs. those with only technique

## Application to Life

### School System Critique
QolarBear's hatred of school:
> "I hate school... it kills the 'interest' that belongs only to youth, that can only be obtained in this life, that cannot be recovered once lost."

Schools destroy "interest" by:
- Prioritizing obedience over curiosity
- Valuing test scores over understanding
- Punishing rule-breaking that serves learning
- Creating a culture of comparison and conformity

### Personal Identity
> "I appreciate people with interest, regardless of their identity, status, or personality... as long as they have 'interest,' they are my friends, true 'living people' in my eyes."

### The Anti-Vulgar Stance
The opposite of "interest" is "vulgarity" (庸俗):
- Vulgarity is accepting mediocrity
- Vulgarity is performing for others' approval
- Vulgarity is losing the ability to appreciate beauty

## The "Interest" Evaluation System

QolarBear is developing a personal evaluation system with "interest" at its core:

### Possible Axioms (Under Development)
1. **Creation Value**: Does this action create or protect "interest"?
2. **Via Negativa**: Does this action reduce vulgarity/mediocrity?
3. **Authenticity**: Is this action driven by internal motivation?

### Application Questions
- Does this action bring me closer to being a "living person"?
- Am I creating beauty or just consuming it?
- Am I following my own path or the path others expect?
