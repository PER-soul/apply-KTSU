# Digital Art Learning Framework

## Core Philosophy: "One Outline, Random Learning, Encoding Summary"

### The Anti-Fragile Art Strategy
Inspired by Taleb's "Anti-Fragile," QolarBear developed a learning approach that embraces randomness while maintaining structure:

> **"One outline, random learning, encoding summary"**
> (一条大纲、随机学习、编码总结)

### The Three Components

#### 1. One Outline (一条大纲)
- A broad, flexible syllabus of topics to cover
- Not rigid curriculum, but general direction
- Examples: Anatomy → Perspective → Color Theory → Composition

#### 2. Random Learning (随机学习)
- Watch various tutorials, read books "opportunistically"
- Follow curiosity rather than strict sequence
- Embrace the "randomness" that builds non-linear neural connections
- **Warning**: Without encoding, this becomes "video-watching pleasure" not "skill retention"

#### 3. Encoding Summary (编码总结)
- Convert learning into named, structured knowledge
- Create mental models that can be articulated
- **Key insight**: "Only knowledge that can be named is prey you've captured"

## Art Learning Principles

### Volume Before Form (先体积后造型)
- First establish volume with geometric blocks
- Then connect with lines to create form
- Prevents "dead-looking" drawings

### Edge Lines as Spatial Folding
- Edge lines (边缘线) are not "outlines"
- They represent where planes turn away from view
- Understanding: "Spatial folding" (空间折叠) - multiple planes compressed into one line

### Hierarchical Modeling
For complex forms (like hands):
1. **Large structure first**: Palm volume and orientation
2. **Medium structures**: Finger groups and major joints
3. **Details**: Individual fingers, wrinkles, textures

### Loomis Method Adaptation
- Traditional Loomis head construction doesn't perfectly fit anime style
- Anime heads are more "egg-shaped" with lower center of gravity
- Adaptation needed: Don't "fit" Loomis, "deconstruct and reconstruct"

## The "Table Learning" vs "Throwing Learning" Balance

### Table Learning (表格式学习)
- Structured, systematic skill acquisition
- Examples: Muscle attachment points, perspective rules
- **When to use**: For fundamentals that require precision

### Throwing Learning (投掷式学习)
- Exploratory, curiosity-driven learning
- Examples: Style exploration, experimental techniques
- **When to use**: For creativity and finding personal voice

### The Danger
Using "throwing learning" to逃避 structured practice:
> "Many people in drawing use 'finding feeling' as an excuse to avoid studying anatomy and perspective. They keep 'throwing' luck, hoping to occasionally draw something good."

**Solution**: Minimum 15 minutes daily of "table learning" as anchor point.

## Kyoto Seika Exam Preparation

### Exam Format
- **Duration**: 90-180 minutes
- **Topics**: Hand + object combinations (classic)
- **Focus**: Observation > Technique

### What Professors Look For
1. **Anatomical accuracy**: Finger thickness, skin texture, vein visibility
2. **Material quality**: Can you show plastic's lightness vs. glass's transparency?
3. **Narrative sense**: Does the pose have "interest"? Does it convey emotion?

### Practice Frequency
- **Minimum**: 2 complete sketches per week
- **Focus**: "Hand holding various objects" (glass ball, crumpled newspaper, water-filled plastic cup)
