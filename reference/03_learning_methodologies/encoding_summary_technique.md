# Encoding Summary Technique

## Core Concept

**Encoding Summary (编码总结)**: The practice of converting learning into named, structured knowledge that can be articulated and recalled.

> **"Only knowledge that can be named is prey you've captured."**

## The Encoding Process

### Step 1: Experience/Input
- Watch a tutorial
- Read a book passage
- Attempt a drawing
- Have a conversation

### Step 2: Pattern Recognition
- What is the underlying principle?
- How does this connect to what I already know?
- What is the "first principle" here?

### Step 3: Naming/Encoding
- Create a descriptive label for the concept
- Use metaphors or visual language
- Make it memorable and specific

### Step 4: Articulation
- Write or speak the concept in your own words
- Connect it to other encoded knowledge
- Create a "knowledge web"

## Benefits of Encoding

1. **Retention**: Named concepts are remembered better
2. **Transfer**: Encoded knowledge applies to new situations
3. **Communication**: Can explain concepts to others (teaching reinforces learning)
4. **Interview Preparation**: Kyoto Seika professors value students who can articulate their thinking
5. **Self-Awareness**: Encoding reveals gaps in understanding

## Common Mistakes

### Surface Encoding
- Using jargon without understanding
- Copying others' labels without internalization
- Creating too many categories (cognitive overload)

### Encoding Without Practice
- "I understand the theory" ≠ "I can execute"
- Encoding must be paired with physical practice (drawing, speaking)
- "Cognitive escape": Using encoding to avoid difficult practice

### Premature Encoding
- Trying to encode before enough exposure
- Some concepts need longer fermentation
- Don't force labels on fuzzy understanding

## The "Fermentation" Connection

Encoding and fermentation work together:
1. **Input**: Raw experience
2. **Fermentation**: Subconscious processing
3. **Emergence**: Insight appears
4. **Encoding**: Capture and name the insight
5. **Integration**: Connect to existing knowledge web
