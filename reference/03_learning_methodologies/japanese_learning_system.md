# Japanese Learning System

## Core Tools

### Anki (SRS - Spaced Repetition System)
- **Purpose**: Vocabulary acquisition and retention
- **Method**: Flashcard-based spaced repetition
- **Logic**: Uses forgetting curve to optimize review timing
- **Daily Target**: 160 words (ideal); 60 words (minimum maintenance)
- **QolarBear's View**: Perfectly aligns with "anti-fragile" philosophy - uses the edge of forgetting to strengthen memory

### Red/Blue Books (红蓝宝书)
- **Red Book**: Vocabulary (N1-N5)
- **Blue Book**: Grammar (N1-N5)
- **Purpose**: Structured, systematic learning
- **Role**: Provides "orderly learning" foundation to counteract anxiety

## Learning Philosophy

### The Anti-Fragile Approach
QolarBear applies Taleb's anti-fragility to language learning:
- **Embrace forgetting**: The SRS algorithm turns forgetting into a strength
- **Volatility is good**: Varying daily word counts (60-260) is acceptable
- **Stress builds resilience**: Difficult words that require multiple reviews create stronger neural connections

### Muscle Memory vs. Contextual Understanding
- **N2 and below**: Can be achieved through structured study
- **N1 and above**: Requires understanding of social context, not just grammar
- **Goal**: Move beyond "correct grammar" to "natural expression"

## Daily Practice Structure

### Minimum Viable Practice
Even on "bad days":
- 10 Anki words (maintains streak and identity)
- 5 minutes of Red/Blue book review

### Ideal Practice
- 160 Anki words (new + review)
- 30 minutes grammar study
- Active immersion (anime, podcasts, music)

### School Environment Adaptation

## Key Principles

1. **Consistency > Intensity**: Better to do 60 words daily than 300 once a week
2. **Input First**: Focus on recognition before production
3. **Context Matters**: Learn words in sentences, not isolation
4. **Embrace the Grind**: Some aspects of language learning are just memorization
5. **Sleep is Study**: Anki reviews are more effective after quality sleep

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- **Perfectionism**: Don't get stuck on one difficult word
- **Comparison**: Don't compare progress to others
- **Skipping Reviews**: The SRS only works if you do the reviews
- **Passive Learning**: Active recall > passive recognition
